Computer Vision has made great strides, especially with the advent of Deepfake, allowing for the creation of increasingly sophisticated fake content, posing new challenges for information authentication in the digital age.

What is Computer Vision

A branch of artificial intelligence that allows computers to "see" and understand the world around them through images and videos.

What is Deepfake

Is a technology that uses artificial intelligence to create realistic fake images and videos, often by replacing one person's face with another person's face.

Role of Computer Vision

Used to analyze features in images and videos, helping to detect abnormalities and determine whether a video is a deepfake or not.

Deepfake detection methods mainly focus on analyzing small details in images and videos, such as eye movements, heartbeats, lighting, and other digital cues to find abnormalities or unnaturalness, thereby determining whether it is a deepfake or not.

Analyze biometric features

Look for abnormalities in heart rate, breathing, eye movement, or facial structure.

Analyze image features

Analyze small details like lighting, shadows, skin texture, or errors in deepfake generation.

Use deep neural networks
